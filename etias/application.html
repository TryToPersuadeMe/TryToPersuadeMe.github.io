ETIAS application process

The ETIAS registration form supposedly will take applicants 10 minutes to fill out. However travelers are advised to complete their application online at least 72 hours prior to their trip. Personal details that should be provided are the following:

  • Full name (as it appears on the passport)
  • Passport number, issuing date and expiration date
  • E-mail
  • Date and place of birth
  • Gender
  • Address
  • Nationality

Also each applicant will be asked a series of security questions.

All the information provided by travelers screened using several security databases such as EURODAC, Visa Information System (VIS) and Schengen Information System (SIS).
When an ETIAS visa waiver has been approved, the applicant will be immediately notified via email. ETIAS visa waiver will be valid for a period of 3 consecutive years, or until the traveler’s passport expires. It allows to visit the EU countries on multiple occasions. Once it has expired, it will no longer be valid. The traveler will need to register online again.

Why ETIAS visa waiver can be denied?

Usually the following reasons could result in the ETIAS visa waiver being refused:
  • The applicant’s passport is not valid
  • The passport used on the ETIAS application was reported lost or stolen
  • The applicant’s name appears on the ETIAS Watchlist
Also it can happen that ETIAS es approved and later revoked. The reasons of such situation can be the following:
  • A traveler has given false personal details or omitting criminal or medical background details.
  • The traveler’s details no longer up to date
  • It is discovered that the traveler previously overstayed the allotted time by a Schengen member state
  • The traveler’s passport is reported lost or stolen

If ETIAS is denied, every traveler will have the right to appeal to the individual Schengen member state that was indicated as the first port of entry. If the appeal is not successful and the applicant believes their treatment has not been fair, they will have the right to request information from the national authority regarding the reasons for their visa denial.
